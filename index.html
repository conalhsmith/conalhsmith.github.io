Scrap Scout - Ongoing

2025

ScrapScout is an autonomous robot designed to detect, classify, and collect litter from beaches using a 3D-printed tank chassis, mechanical collection system, and sensor fusion for navigation. It will be powered by a Raspberry Pi 5, and integrates a custom computer vision pipeline with real-time deep learning-based litter detection. A dataset of 7,311 images with eight classes and segmentation masks was created. Two methods were tested: an optimized YOLOv8n-seg model (mAP@50-95: 0.394 @ 6.73 FPS) and a hybrid Faster R-CNN with VIAM segmentation with the aim of decreasing computational costs (8.20 FPS, lower accuracy). A Kalman filter maintains object tracking, and spatial analysis estimates real-world litter positions. Ongoing improvements target detection accuracy and real-time performance. The primary focus at this stage is printing the mechanical chassis and finishing the computer vision pipeline.

Robocup

2024

The 2024 Robocop competition follows a Harry Potter theme and resembles a mechatronics version of quidditch. Teams of 3 are required to design and build autonomous robots to compete in a knockout competition at the end of the year. The goal of each round is to collect the largest number of weights, points being doubled if weights are delivered to the robot’s home base. The snitch may also be caught for bonus points. The robots must navigate an arena, littered with obstacles and traps such as fake weights and speedbumps. Collecting dummy weights will result in a penalty. Rounds are short, consisting of only 2 minutes meaning that weight detection, collection, and primary areas for teams to focus on.

I was primarily responsbile for concept generation, and mechanical design. I was able to design and build a efficient weight collection system using a paddle and a ramp, testing concluding that it successfully collected weights 90% of the time. This design featured a continuity circuit for real/fake weight differentiation and a simple release gate to drop wreights at base. After concluding the mechanical design I designed the weight collection software, and assisted with navigational software (SLAM).

Key achievements:

Challenges included kernel integration and simulator-to-hardware transition. The final system successfully demonstrated:
